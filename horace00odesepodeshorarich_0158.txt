10. regius goes with lacessat i.e. let him not provoke in his royal pride ;
regius almost equals superbus.

11, 12. cruenta 1 ...caedesz for the grouping see on P. 7.

medias (ps) i.e. right through, in the very midst of. Compare Odes
3. 16. 9 etc.

14. fugacem (ps): the et preceding adds to the emphasis of the epithet.
See too on P. 21.

15. imbellis (p) echoes fugacem i.e. even if he runs away.

imbellis iuventae: the preposited genitive is the real object of par-cim
see on P. 38.

16. timido: for its position see on P. 33.

17. sordidae is an emphatic addendum (see on P. 53); Virtue may be
repulsed, but never dishonourably.

18. intaminatis (ps) is contrasted with the preceding sordidae. See too
P. 21.

20. arbitrio...aurae is an emphatic addendum (see on P. 53).

popularis (p): for genet-ic adjectives preposited see on P. 37.

22. negata (ps) i.e. ‘though closed'3 Virtue pays no attention to the
sign-boards of the conventional world.

23. udam (ps) equals ‘misty,’ ‘dank'; contrast liquidum (p) aethera at
Odes 2. 20. 2. See also l'. 21.
