V. The order of this Ode is osten strained. Is it a translation?

1. multa gracilis...puer in rosa: for the grouping see P. 10.

te puer: see on Odes 1. 2. 17.

2. liquidis (βψ i.e. (drenched) with the liquid of scents (see Ρ. 27); but
liquidis...odoribus may be grouped with urget also i.e. courts thee with scents
(see Ρ. 21 and 23).

3. grato (μὴτ the order is, perhaps, on the analogy of grato Pyrrhae
(dat.) sub antro. For the intervening vocative compare Odes 1. 1. 1, 1. 7. 19,
1. 9. 8, 1. 17. 10, 1. 32. 4, 2.1.14‚ 2.13.27, 3. 2. 1, 3. 4. 2, 3. 13. 2, 4. 1. 1 (?)‚
4. 1. 40, 4. 5. 1, 4. 5. 5, 4. 14. 3, 4. 15. 4 Epod. 5. 73.

4. flavam (ps): golden hair would be the special beauty of a lady named
Pyrrha. See also Ρ. 21, and compare Odes 3. 9. 19.

5. simplex munditiis: an addendum3 see on P. 53.

5, 6. fidem Ι mutatosque deos: for the position of mutatos see on
P. 33.

6, 7. aspera Ι nigris aequora ventis: for the grouping see P. 9.

8. insolens i.e. ὅτε ἀήθης div—because he is unused to them.

9. credulus aurea: an emphatic addendum; see P. 53. A Roman reads
the words thus: ‘Fool to trust her flash of goldl’ Compare odes 1. 6. 9,
1. 13. 14.

For the grouping qui...te...credulus aurea see P. 16 ad fin.

nunc has stress by separation sromfruilur: Horace could have made the

11. sperat has stress; see Odes 4. 9. 26.

nescius sc. ὤν i.e. ‘because he knows not’-an addendum3 see P. 53.

12. fallacis has stress; see Odes 4. 9. 26. He knows the breeze but not
